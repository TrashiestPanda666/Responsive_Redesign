T-34B Mentor

The T-34 was the brainchild of Walter Beech, who developed it as the Beechcraft Model 45 private venture at a time when there was no defense budget for a new trainer model.

A-4C Skyhawk

The A-4D2N (A-4C) on display was built in El Segundo CA in 1959. It was delivered to the US Navy at Patuxent Naval Air Station in Maryland and was flight tested there on November 11, 1959.

F-14A Tomcat

Grumman F-14 Tomcat was designed as the successor to the F-4 Phantom in the fleet defense roll. It was designed to destroy enemy aircraft at extreme range before they become a threat.

E-2B Hawkeye

The E-2 on display was delivered to the US Navy as an E-2A model in September 1966 and later upgraded to the E-2B standard in January 1970 after undergoing a complete upgrade.

RH-53D Sea Stallion

Our RH-53 was delivered to the US Navy on February 28, 1973 and was assigned to Narostrat training facility from May to November 1973. It was stationed at Norfolk Virginia Air Station.

P-80A Shooting Star

The Shooting Star was the first USAF aircraft to exceed 500 mph in flight, the first American jet airplane manufactured in large quantities, and the first USAF jet used in combat.
